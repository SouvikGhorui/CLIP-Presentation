Dual Encoder
Architecture

CLIP uses two separate neural networks to encode images and text into a shared embedding space of dimension $d$.

Image Encoder — ViT or ResNet

Processes the image $x_i$ through a vision backbone, then projects to the shared space:

$$\mathbf{I}_i = \frac{W_I \cdot f_I(x_i)}{\| W_I \cdot f_I(x_i) \|}$$

Text Encoder — Transformer

Processes the text $t_j$ through a causal Transformer, then projects and normalizes:

$$\mathbf{T}_j = \frac{W_T \cdot f_T(t_j)}{\| W_T \cdot f_T(t_j) \|}$$

L2 NORMALIZATION

Both embeddings live on the unit hypersphere, so $\|\mathbf{I}_i\| = \|\mathbf{T}_j\| = 1$. This means dot product $=$ cosine similarity.
