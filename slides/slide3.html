The Contrastive
Loss (InfoNCE)

CLIP's training objective maximizes similarity for correct pairs while minimizing it for incorrect pairs within each batch.

1. Cosine Similarity

$$\text{sim}(\mathbf{I}_i, \mathbf{T}_j) = \mathbf{I}_i^{\top} \mathbf{T}_j$$

Since both are L2-normalized, this equals cosine similarity.

2. Temperature-Scaled Logits

$$\ell_{ij} = \frac{\mathbf{I}_i^{\top} \mathbf{T}_j}{\tau}$$

The learnable temperature $\tau$ sharpens or softens the distribution.

3. Softmax over Negatives

For each image $i$, treat the correct text as positive and all other $N-1$ texts as negatives.

IMAGE-TO-TEXT LOSS (InfoNCE)

$$\mathcal{L}_i^{I \to T} = -\log \frac{\exp(\mathbf{I}_i^{\top}\mathbf{T}_i / \tau)}{\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{N} \exp(\mathbf{I}_i^{\top}\mathbf{T}_k / \tau)}$$
