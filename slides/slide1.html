CLIP: Contrastive
Language-Image
Pre-training

CLIP learns to connect images and natural language by training on 400 million image-text pairs scraped from the internet.

The core intuition: instead of predicting a fixed set of labels, learn a general joint embedding space where related images and texts are pulled close together.

The Key Idea

Given a batch of $N$ image-text pairs, train two encoders:

$$f_I: \text{Image} \to \mathbb{R}^d, \quad f_T: \text{Text} \to \mathbb{R}^d$$

so that matched pairs have high cosine similarity, while unmatched pairs have low similarity.
